## Feature shipped — `feat(betb2b)` `fbe09c3`

- `src/sites/betb2b/storage.py` — `dump_json` (auto-compress >64 KiB or any
  `.gz` path; `compress=True/False` forces), `load_json` (gzip detected by
  magic bytes, extension-agnostic), `compress_file`/`decompress_file`.
  Deterministic gzip (no embedded filename, `mtime=0`).
- CLI: `scrape --compress`, new `view <file> [--compact|--decompress-to]`.
- `validate_live --compress` (captures gzip; summary stays plain).
- README "Saving & viewing output" section. 9 tests.

## Recommendation on the user's question

**Compress the large files, not the small ones.** `dump_json(compress=None)`
already does this automatically (64 KiB threshold). gzip (stdlib, universal,
streamable) over brotli (needs a dep). Everything reads back through
`load_json`, so compression never leaks into the rest of the scraper, and
`betb2b view` gives a one-command human view of any file.
